About

Conrad Reynoldson officially founded Washington Civil & Disability Advocate in 2016. He was born in Tacoma and is a lifelong Washingtonian. Conrad attended college at Seattle Pacific University and graduated Summa Cum Laude with a double major in History and Political Science in 2009. He graduated from law school at the University of Washington School of Law in 2014. Conrad is admitted to practice in Washington State - both the Western and Eastern Districts, and is a Blackstone Legal Fellow.

Mr. Reynoldson is currently Chair of the Employer Awards Subcommittee and a member of the Coordinating Committee of the Governor's Committee on Disability Issues and Employment. Conrad is committed to providing low or no cost legal services to individuals in need of protection of their civil rights with a particular passion for helping clients be a part of significantly increasing accessibility in the state of Washington.

Accessibility

WACDA has a particular emphasis on protecting disability rights and ensuring accessibility for all.

Disability Civil Rights

WACDA has a strong background in disability civil rights and stays current with legal precedents and federal and state laws.

Freedom of Religion and Speech

WACDA is also committed to protecting civil rights more broadly and has experience in free speech and religious liberty.
